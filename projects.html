Conway's Game of Life

Conway's Game of Life is a cellular automaton devised by mathematician John Conway in 1970. It is a zero-player game, meaning that its progression is determined by its initial state. The universe of the Game of Life is an infinite two-dimensional grid of square cells, each of which can be in one of two states: alive or dead. The state of each cell evolves over time based on simple rules. Here you can check it out!

Game of Life's Variation

This project is a variation of Conway's Game of Life with additional rules and features.

It was my midterm project on the Python courses in my Home country.

Additional Rules:

  • If the number of aliens is bigger in a range of 1 cell (3x3 active field), human has a 33% chance to survive, 20% chance to be caught and become an alien, and otherwise, he dies.
  • If the number of people is bigger or equal, the alien has a 50% chance to die in a battle.
  • If the number of aliens on the neighbor cells is more than 2, 33% chance that one more alien will be born.
  • If the number of people on the neighbor cells is more than 1, 80% chance that one more human will be born.
